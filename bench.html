This is an inquisitive work, loosely intended to serve the physical function of a bench. It is an object that speculates on aesthetic value systems outside of those defined by Western/Christian aesthetic principle as “good.” Ex. the contemporary infatuation with the ideal form and the erasure of human participation in the process of manifesting them. The “bench” turns to and is informed by the teleologies of various indigenous groups employing a sympathetic knowledge of the landscape and an embrace of decay in their conception of reality.

The images displayed here represent only the physical outcome of a project which ultimately privileged research, writing and conversation over finished pieces. This process helped me develop a deeper—yet far from complete—understanding of making in my physical and temporal context.

Finally, there are many failures evident in this project and its process is undoubtedly tarnished by the distance between me and those I am trying to learn from. Even the nature of approaching this build in the framework of a “project”—something I can step in and out of—reflects the very forces of alienation which my world view has been molded by and which I am trying to address.
